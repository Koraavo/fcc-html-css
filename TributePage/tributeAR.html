A.R.Rahman

Mozart of Madras

Timeline

  • A.R.Rahman was born on Jan 6, 1967 in Madras, India as A.S.Dileep Kumar.

  • He is a two-time winner and five-times nominee of the Academy Award.

  • He has sold more than 150 million copies of his work in multiple languages. He mastered the keyboard, piano, synthesizer, harmonium and guitar, and was particularly interested in the synthesizer because it was the "ideal combination of music and technology"

  • He has a degree in western classical music from Trinity College of Music, London

  • Set up his own studio called Panchathan Record-Inn at Chennai

  • Converted to Islam at the age of 23 with other family members in 1989, changing his name to Allah Rakha Rahman (A. R. Rahman)

  • 1991

    Musical career in movies started with Roja

    - National Award for the soundtrack

    - listed on Time's all-time "10 Best Soundtracks" in 2005

  • 2001

    Andrew Lloyd Webber invited Rahman to compose for his musical, Bombay Dreams

  • 2005

    Composed the score for the stage production of Lord of the Rings

    - Several Tracks featured in movies such as ‘Lord of War’, ‘Inside Man’, and the ‘Accidental Husband’

  • 2008

    Slumdog Millionaire got him two Oscars, Best Score and Best Song, 15 other awards including two Grammys, the Golden Globe and the BAFTA

  • 2009

    Time Magazine named him as one of the 100 most influential people in the world.

  • 2010

    Rahman received the Padma Bhushan, India's third-highest civilian honour

  • 2011

    His work in 127 Hours won him Golden Globe, BAFTA, and two Academy Award nominations (Best Original Music Score and Best Original Song)


    Collaborated with other international artists including Nusrat Fateh Ali Khan, Michael Jackson, Michael Bolton, MIA, Vanessa Mae, the Pussycat Dolls, Sarah Brightman, Dido, Hossam Ramzy, Hans Zimmer and Akon.
